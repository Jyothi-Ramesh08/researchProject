Procedure

Using a numbered list, describe your procedure (include pictures)


Step

Description

Image

Step 1
Conduct a mini-experiment to find out the concentrations of E.coli K-12 that should be used. Do this by exposing the planaria to different amounts of E.coli K-12 to find the emounts that they do not die from

Step 2
Using an innoculating loop, take a sample of E.coli K-12 from a stab culture. Than add it to a test tube with 10mL of Luria Broth, swirl it, and let it incubate in a water bath.

Step 3
Put planaria on ice to slow movement down its movement. Then, using a scalpel, cut planaria transversely, and measure lengths before and after being cut using a microscope

Step 4
Add the appropriate amount of E.coli K-12 (0ml, 1.5ml, 15ml, 150ml, and 300ml) to each Petri dish of planaria using a micropipette

Step 5
Allow planaria to regenerate for as many days as necessary until fully regenerated. Regeneration is measured by formation of physical characteristics.
